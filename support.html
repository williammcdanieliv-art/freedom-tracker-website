The most common cause is that the action words in your broker's CSV aren't recognized. Check the red "Skipped row X:" messages in the preview — they show the exact word the importer couldn't classify (e.g., "Market Buy", "Dividend Payment", "Stock Transfer").

Try these fixes in order:
1. Select your specific brokerage from the dropdown instead of Auto-detect.
2. If your broker isn't in the list, try a similar one (e.g., TD Ameritrade transactions often work with the Schwab profile since the merger).
3. If neither works, report the unrecognised action word and we'll add it in the next update.

Freedom Tracker accepts standard US ticker symbols (letters, numbers, dots, and dashes). Some brokerages export Berkshire Hathaway as BRK/B with a forward slash, which is flagged as invalid.

The fix: after your import, manually add BRK.B (with a dot) using the quick-add form at the top of the screen, and enter those purchases by hand. Most brokers use BRK.B which imports without issue.

Interactive Brokers' "Activity Statement" CSV contains multiple sections (Trades, Dividends, Fees, etc.), each with its own header row. Freedom Tracker reads from the first header it finds, so only one section imports at a time.

The solution: in IBKR's report settings, generate separate CSV exports for Trades and for Dividends, then import each file individually. This gives you clean results for both transaction types.
No — pick one approach, not both.

Freedom Tracker's DRIP toggle rebuilds reinvestment history from scratch using Yahoo Finance's dividend history. It calculates how many shares you'd have accumulated if every dividend was reinvested at the ex-dividend price.

Broker DRIP transactions imported from CSV are recorded as real buy transactions (shares added to your buys list).

If you do both, the DRIP toggle will calculate reinvestment on top of the already-imported shares, resulting in double-counted shares and inflated income numbers.

Recommendation: Enable the DRIP toggle (automatic, Yahoo-based) and skip the DRIP rows from your broker import. Or import the broker DRIP rows and leave the toggle off. Either works — just not both.
The income figures are based on Yahoo Finance's dividend history. This shows $0 when:

1. Yahoo Finance is temporarily unavailable. Press F5 to refresh. If prices load but income is still $0, the dividend data wasn't returned.

2. The ticker symbol doesn't match Yahoo's symbol. For example, some ETFs trade under a slightly different symbol on Yahoo than on your brokerage. Check finance.yahoo.com to confirm the exact symbol.

3. No dividends were paid in the last 12 months. If a fund recently cut its dividend, the trailing 12-month figure will be zero or very low.

4. You own the shares as of today but with a future purchase date. Make sure the date you entered is the actual past purchase date, not a future date.
💾 Your Data
Your portfolio is stored in a file called freedom_portfolio.json in the same folder as the Freedom Tracker application — entirely on your computer. Nothing is ever sent to a server.

To find it on Windows, look in the folder where you installed the app (typically C:\Users\YourName\AppData\Local\Programs\FreedomTracker\).

The quickest way to create a portable backup: use 📤 Export CSV from the toolbar — it saves everything (holdings, transactions, settings) to a CSV file you can store anywhere.
There is no in-app undo for deletions. If you have a backup, restore it:

If you used "📤 Export CSV" before the deletion: Click "📥 Import CSV" and select your backup file. Your full portfolio is restored.

If you have a copy of freedom_portfolio.json: Replace the current file in the app's folder with your backup copy, then restart Freedom Tracker.

If you have no backup: The data is gone. We recommend exporting a backup after any major changes. Going forward, consider a quick weekly export to a cloud folder.
Yes. One license covers all computers you personally own. To use your portfolio on both machines:

1. Export your portfolio on machine A using 📤 Export CSV.
2. Copy the file to machine B (USB drive, email, cloud storage — any method works).
3. On machine B, use 📥 Import CSV to load it.

Note: changes made on one computer don't sync automatically to the other. Export from whichever machine you last updated and import on the other to stay current.
Freedom Tracker fetches live prices from Yahoo Finance. If prices show "—":

1. Check your internet connection. Press F5 to retry.
2. Yahoo Finance may be temporarily slow or down. This happens occasionally — try again in a few minutes.
3. A firewall or VPN may be blocking the connection. Try disabling your VPN temporarily.

Your portfolio data is unaffected — prices are fetched on demand and not required for the app to open. Income projections update as soon as prices load successfully.
